About ITC

Information-theoretic cryptography studies security against computationally unbounded adversaries. It includes protocols with unconditional guarantees, as well as information-theoretic methods used to understand computational and quantum security.

The conference provides a dedicated venue for rigorous work in this area and encourages sustained exchange among neighboring research communities.

Research scope

Topics represented at ITC

The scope is intentionally broad; related mathematical and computational work is welcome.

Foundations and proofs

  • Randomness extraction and privacy amplification
  • Information-theoretic proof systems and reductions
  • Lower bounds and black-box separations
  • Information-theoretic analysis of symmetric-key cryptography

Privacy and secure computation

  • Secret sharing and secure multi-party computation
  • Differential privacy
  • Private information retrieval and locally decodable codes
  • Oblivious data structures

Codes, channels, and resilience

  • Authentication and non-malleable codes
  • Adversarial and noisy channels
  • Bounded-storage cryptography
  • Physical-layer security

Quantum and idealized models

  • Quantum cryptography with unconditional security
  • Random-oracle and generic-group models
  • Tamper-proof hardware and ideal obfuscation
  • New information-theoretic primitives and computational analogues
